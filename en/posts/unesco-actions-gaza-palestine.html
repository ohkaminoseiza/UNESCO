UNESCO's Actions in Gaza and Palestine

April 12, 2026 | Human Rights, Education

Supporting the Foundations of Recovery through Education, Culture, and Media

In Gaza and Palestine, ongoing conflict has severely damaged the foundations of society, including education, culture, and the media. Opportunities to learn, cultural heritage that carries collective memory, and the conditions needed for reliable reporting have all been deeply affected.

In response, UNESCO has been working on both emergency relief and long-term recovery. Its efforts focus on three key areas: ensuring the continuity of education, protecting cultural heritage, and supporting journalism.

According to UNESCO, as of April 2026, the organization had mobilized 5.7 million US dollars in emergency assistance for Gaza. This support aims to restore access to education, safeguard cultural heritage, and sustain the media sector. In education, temporary learning spaces and online initiatives such as the Gaza Virtual University Campus have been developed so that students can continue learning despite the disruption.

In the field of culture, significant damage has been reported to historical sites and cultural institutions across Gaza. UNESCO has been supporting damage assessment, emergency protection, and preparations for future recovery. Protecting cultural heritage is not simply about conserving the past. It is also a way of preserving memory and identity, and of supporting the long-term recovery of communities.

UNESCO is also working to support journalists and the continuation of independent reporting. Through training, equipment, and psychosocial support, the organization seeks to help media professionals continue their work under extremely difficult conditions. Reliable information is a public foundation: it enables people to understand their situation, make informed decisions, and maintain social trust during times of crisis.

Another important initiative is UNESCO's support for curriculum renewal in Palestine. In April 2026, UNESCO worked with Palestinian education officials and curriculum specialists to advance a new curriculum framework that emphasizes peace, human rights, and sustainable development. This effort goes beyond updating educational content. It reflects a longer-term vision of how education can help shape the future of society.

UNESCO has also extended support to Lebanon, where conflict has displaced many children and young people. Approximately 650,000 US dollars has been allocated to support the continuity of education for displaced populations, including learning opportunities and psychosocial support. This reflects UNESCO's consistent position that education remains a fundamental human right, even in times of crisis.

Taken together, UNESCO's actions are not limited to immediate humanitarian assistance. By protecting education, culture, and information, the organization is helping to rebuild the foundations of society and lay the groundwork for future peace.

Conflict destroys not only physical infrastructure, but also less visible elements such as learning, memory, and trust. For this reason, sustaining education, protecting culture, and supporting reliable information are essential steps toward recovery.
