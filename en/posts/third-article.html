A New Paradigm in Human Rights Education

March 15, 2026 | Human Rights, Education

What approaches are possible at the school education level to achieve an inclusive society? Drawing on domestic and international case studies, we explore the future of human rights education.

1. Where Inclusive Education Stands Today

Progress has been made in creating environments where everyone can learn together, regardless of disability or background, but many psychological and physical barriers still remain.

2. Human Rights as a Personal Issue

Rather than memorizing human rights issues as distant, abstract knowledge, we need a perspective that reframes them as questions of "difference and fairness" present in the everyday classroom and in our own lives.

3. Designing a Dialogue-Centered Classroom

The very process of engaging in dialogue with others from different backgrounds, overcoming differences of opinion, and reaching consensus is itself the most effective form of human rights learning.
