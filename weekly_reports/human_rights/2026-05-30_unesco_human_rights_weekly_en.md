# UNESCO Human Rights Weekly Report 2026-05-30

## Overview

During the week of 23–30 May 2026, UNESCO issued a significant statement condemning attacks on cultural and educational facilities in Ukraine and published a landmark Global Education Monitoring (GEM) Report connecting education to justice and human rights. The UNESCO Statement of 25 May condemned aerial strikes on Ukrainian cultural heritage sites, while the GEM Report released on 26 May argued that education is fundamental to achieving justice for the 1.4 billion people worldwide who lack meaningful access to legal recourse. These activities reinforce UNESCO's core mission to protect cultural property in conflict and to advance human rights through education.

## UNESCO Statement: Condemning Strikes on Cultural and Educational Institutions in Ukraine

**Date:** 25 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO Statement – 25 May 2026](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-statement-25-may-2026)

UNESCO expressed deep alarm at reported aerial strikes in the Kyiv region of Ukraine that resulted in severe damage to more than 10 cultural institutions and 2 educational and research facilities, including the National Academy of Music of Ukraine, the Central Post Office, and the Building of the Ministry of Foreign Affairs. UNESCO condemned these attacks as violations of international law, which protects cultural property, educational institutions, students, education personnel, and media professionals. UNESCO stated it stands ready to support relevant authorities in assessing damage and identifying urgent measures within its mandate.

## Education and Justice: Learning to Build Just Societies — GEM Report 2026

**Date:** 26 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO Global Education Monitoring Report](https://www.unesco.org/gem-report/en)

The UNESCO Global Education Monitoring (GEM) Report, published jointly with Namati, presented a landmark study arguing that education is essential for achieving justice at a global scale. The report found that approximately 1.4 billion people lack meaningful access to justice because they cannot resolve everyday legal problems, while inequalities, discrimination, conflict, and climate-related pressures are intensifying this gap. Education is identified as a crucial means of helping people understand their rights, navigate legal systems, and participate fully in society, while also reducing vulnerability to exploitation and violence.

## Q&A: What You Need to Know about How Education Can Help Build More Just Societies

**Date:** 26 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO – Q&A: What you need to know about how education can help build more just societies](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/qa-what-you-need-know-about-how-education-can-help-build-more-just-societies)

Published alongside the GEM Report, this Q&A article explains in accessible terms how education can help build more just societies. The article highlights that girls, ethnic minorities, migrants, displaced populations, and children with disabilities are most likely to face barriers to both education and justice simultaneously. It argues that investing in inclusive education reduces long-term social inequality and lowers vulnerability to exploitation and violence, making it a fundamental human rights imperative.
