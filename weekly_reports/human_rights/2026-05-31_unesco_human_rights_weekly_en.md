# UNESCO Human Rights Weekly Report 2026-05-31

## Overview

The week of May 24–31, 2026 was an active one for UNESCO on human rights. Key activities included two condemnation statements regarding Russian attacks on educational facilities in Ukraine, a press freedom conference in South Sudan ahead of December elections, the launch of an anti-online-misogyny campaign in South Africa, and the UNESCO Culture and Arts Education Week (May 25–31). Together, these activities reflect UNESCO's broad engagement with human rights across multiple dimensions: protection of education in conflict zones, freedom of expression, digital rights, gender equality, and the right to cultural participation.

---

## UNESCO Statement: Alarm Over Attacks in the Kyiv Region

**Date:** May 25, 2026

UNESCO expressed deep alarm regarding reported aerial strikes in the Kyiv region amid the ongoing escalation of Russia's war against Ukraine. The statement condemned attacks against civilian infrastructure, reaffirmed the obligations of all parties under international humanitarian law to protect civilians and civilian facilities, and called for an immediate end to attacks on educational, cultural, and media institutions. UNESCO continues to document and verify damage to cultural heritage and educational facilities in Ukraine.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-statement-may-25-2026](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-statement-may-25-2026)

---

## UNESCO Statement: Severe Damage to Vocational School in Starobilsk, Luhansk Region

**Date:** 25 May 2026

UNESCO voiced alarm over reports of severe damage to a vocational school and dormitory in Starobilsk in Ukraine's Luhansk region, with civilian casualties reportedly including students. UNESCO condemned attacks against cultural property, educational institutions, students, education personnel, and media professionals under international law, calling on all parties to uphold their obligations under international humanitarian law. The organization reiterated its commitment to documenting violations of the right to education in conflict settings.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-statement-25-may-2026](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-statement-25-may-2026)

---

## Shaping a Future at Peace: Press Freedom Conference in South Sudan Explores Media-Security Cooperation

**Date:** May 25, 2026

UNESCO organized a press freedom conference in South Sudan to explore cooperation between the media and security sectors ahead of the country's December 2026 elections. Participants from media, government, and security institutions discussed journalist safety, access to information, censorship, and the vital role of a free press in supporting democratic processes and peace. The conference was made possible through support from Norwegian People's Aid and the Japan International Cooperation Agency (JICA), and marked a UNESCO commitment to working alongside South Sudanese institutions to build a safer information environment.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/shaping-future-peace-press-freedom-conference-south-sudan-explores-media-security-cooperation-lead](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/shaping-future-peace-press-freedom-conference-south-sudan-explores-media-security-cooperation-lead)

---

## Build or Break: New Campaign Challenges Young Men to Reject Online Misogyny in South Africa

**Date:** May 25, 2026

The "Build or Break" campaign launched in South Africa as part of UNESCO's Social Media 4 Peace (SM4P) initiative, funded by the European Union and led by the Centre for Analytics and Behavioural Change (CABC). Under the theme "Reclaiming Digital Spaces: Safety. Dignity. Equality.", the campaign directly addresses young men and boys, challenging them to reject online misogyny and technology-facilitated gender-based violence (tfGBV). The campaign promotes a vision of masculinity grounded in integrity and respect, countering narratives that equate violence with strength.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/build-or-break-new-campaign-challenges-young-men-reject-online-misoginy-south-africa](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/build-or-break-new-campaign-challenges-young-men-reject-online-misoginy-south-africa)

---

## UNESCO Culture and Arts Education Week (May 25–31, 2026)

**Dates:** May 25–31, 2026

Following a decision at UNESCO's 43rd General Conference in 2025 to rename what was previously International Arts Education Week, the first UNESCO Culture and Arts Education Week ran May 25–31, 2026. The week highlights the transformative power of culture and the arts in building inclusive, peaceful, and sustainable societies, emphasizing their role in inspiring creativity, fostering empathy, and supporting lifelong learning. The week underscores the human right to cultural participation as an essential dimension of quality education.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/weeks/arts-education](https://www.unesco.org/en/weeks/arts-education)
