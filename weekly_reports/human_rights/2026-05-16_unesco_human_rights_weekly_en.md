# UNESCO Human Rights Weekly Report 2026-05-16

## Overview

During the week of May 9–16, 2026, international coverage continued to build on UNESCO's landmark World Press Freedom Day 2026 events held May 3–5 in Lusaka, Zambia. The week was dominated by follow-up reporting on the UNESCO 2026 World Trends Report on Press Freedom, which documented a 10% global decline in press freedom and a 48% rise in efforts to control media. The UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize was awarded to the Sudanese Journalists Syndicate in recognition of their work under conflict. UNESCO's Dakar office convened West African and Sahelian media stakeholders on May 5. Collectively, these developments reflect UNESCO's sustained effort to defend freedom of expression, protect journalists, and uphold the human right to access information — particularly in conflict-affected and authoritarian contexts.

---

## World Press Freedom Day 2026 Conference: "Shaping a Future at Peace"

**Date:** May 4–5, 2026 (follow-up reporting ongoing through the week of May 9–16)

UNESCO convened the World Press Freedom Day 2026 international conference on May 4–5 in Lusaka, Zambia, under the theme "Shaping a Future at Peace: Promoting Press Freedom for Human Rights, Development, and Security." More than 300 participants from Africa and beyond gathered to address growing threats to freedom of expression and information ecosystem integrity worldwide. Key outcomes included the launch of the 6th edition of the Southern Africa Press Freedom Report 2026 by the Media Institute of Southern Africa (MISA), and the establishment of the African Union Group of Friends on the Safety of Journalists. On May 5, approximately 150 Zambian students participated in the Academic Conference on the Safety of Journalists.

## UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize 2026 Awarded to Sudanese Journalists Syndicate

**Date:** May 4, 2026

UNESCO awarded the 2026 Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize to the Sudanese Journalists Syndicate, recognizing the organization's courageous and sustained documentation of journalist killings, media violations, and press repression amid Sudan's ongoing armed conflict. The Syndicate has recorded 32 journalist deaths, 556 violations against media workers, and the closure of numerous newspapers and radio stations — making Sudan one of the most dangerous countries for journalists. The award ceremony was held at UNESCO Headquarters in Paris on May 4. Created in 1997, the prize — the only United Nations award for journalists — carries a USD $25,000 prize.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco/guillermo-cano-world-press-freedom-prize-2026-awarded-sudanese-journalists-syndicate](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco/guillermo-cano-world-press-freedom-prize-2026-awarded-sudanese-journalists-syndicate)

## UNESCO 2026 World Trends Report on Press Freedom: A 10% Global Decline

**Date:** May 3, 2026 (released for World Press Freedom Day; widely referenced this week)

UNESCO's 2026 World Trends Report on Press Freedom documents a 10% global decline in press freedom and a 48% increase in efforts to control or restrict media worldwide. Violence against journalists remains pervasive, with 85% of killings going unpunished. Independent media face mounting economic fragility driven by the concentration of over 54% of global advertising revenues within digital platforms. The situation is especially acute in the Sahel region, where military authorities in Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger have intensified media repression through abductions, forced conscription of journalists, and broadcast shutdowns; approximately 214 press freedom violations were recorded in West Africa alone from January 2024 to December 2025.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/press-freedom-declines-globally-experts-warn-2026-world-press-freedom-day-conference-zambia](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/press-freedom-declines-globally-experts-warn-2026-world-press-freedom-day-conference-zambia)

## UNESCO Dakar Celebrates World Press Freedom Day with West African Media Stakeholders

**Date:** May 5, 2026

UNESCO's Dakar Regional Office organized a gathering of media stakeholders from Western Africa and the Sahel on May 5, 2026, to promote press freedom, information integrity, and regional media development. The event was conducted in partnership with Democracy Radio and highlighted the crucial role of women's voices and peace journalism in the region. Against a backdrop of severe military crackdowns on independent media in several Sahelian states, the gathering underscored UNESCO's commitment to supporting journalists and building resilient media ecosystems in the region.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-dakar-celebrates-world-press-freedom-day-west-africa-democracy-radio-highlighting-women-peace](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-dakar-celebrates-world-press-freedom-day-west-africa-democracy-radio-highlighting-women-peace)
