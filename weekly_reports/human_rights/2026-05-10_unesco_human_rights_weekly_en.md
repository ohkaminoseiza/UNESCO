# UNESCO Human Rights Weekly Report 2026-05-10

## Overview

The week of May 3–10, 2026 was dominated by World Press Freedom Day (May 3), one of UNESCO's most significant annual events linking press freedom directly to human rights. The 2026 Global Conference, held May 4–5 in Lusaka, Zambia, convened journalists, policymakers, technologists, and civil society under the theme "Shaping a Future at Peace: Promoting Press Freedom for Human Rights, Development, and Security." The 2026 UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize was awarded to the Sudanese Journalists Syndicate in recognition of its courageous documentation of journalist killings and media violations amid Sudan's ongoing conflict. UNESCO's flagship report released this week warned of a historic 10% global decline in freedom of expression between 2012 and 2024, underscoring the urgency of collective action to protect journalists and uphold the right to information worldwide.

## World Press Freedom Day 2026 Global Conference: "Shaping a Future at Peace"

**Date:** May 4–5, 2026

UNESCO convened the World Press Freedom Day 2026 Global Conference at the Mulungushi International Conference Centre in Lusaka, Zambia. Under the theme "Shaping a Future at Peace: Promoting Press Freedom for Human Rights, Development, and Security," the conference brought together journalists, digital rights advocates, technologists, policymakers, civil society organizations, academics, researchers, educators, youth leaders, and content creators. Key sessions examined journalism in conflict and crisis contexts, the impact of AI and algorithms on press freedom, journalist safety, and the fight against impunity. The conference aimed to move beyond diagnosis toward coordinated action, aligning journalism, technology, and human rights actors around practical strategies for stronger information ecosystems.

## UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize 2026 Awarded to Sudanese Journalists Syndicate

**Date:** May 4–5, 2026

UNESCO awarded the 2026 UNESCO/Guillermo Cano World Press Freedom Prize to the Sudanese Journalists Syndicate at a ceremony in Paris attended by Sudanese journalists from across Europe. Since fighting broke out in Sudan in 2023, the Syndicate has documented 32 journalist deaths and 556 violations against media workers, while also recording the closure of numerous newspapers and radio stations. Despite operating under extreme danger, the Syndicate has continued to provide vital, reliable information to the public and advocate for journalist safety. The prize includes a certificate of recognition and a monetary award of USD $25,000. The ceremony served as both a show of solidarity and recognition of resilience during one of the most challenging periods for Sudanese journalism.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco/guillermo-cano-world-press-freedom-prize-2026-awarded-sudanese-journalists-syndicate

## UNESCO's World Press Freedom Day 2026 Report: Historic Decline in Press Freedom Globally

**Date:** May 3–5, 2026

UNESCO released its flagship report on World Press Freedom Day, warning that freedom of expression has experienced a historic 10% decline globally between 2012 and 2024. The report identifies an alarming rise in self-censorship by journalists and a sharp increase in physical and online attacks against media workers as key drivers of this decline. UNESCO called on Member States, civil society, and media organizations to strengthen legal protections for journalists, tackle impunity for crimes against media workers, and address the systemic threats posed by AI-enabled disinformation and media concentration.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unescos-world-press-freedom-day-2026-spotlights-continued-decline-press-freedom-globally

## International Federation of Journalists Centenary Congress — Paris

**Date:** May 5–7, 2026

The International Federation of Journalists (IFJ) held its Centenary Congress in Paris under the theme "100 years of international solidarity for strong journalism and trade unionism." UNESCO joined in marking this milestone, reaffirming its commitment to journalist safety, editorial independence, and the protection of media workers globally. The congress served as a platform for renewing collective commitments to press freedom and human rights in an era of growing threats to independent journalism.
