# UNESCO Environment Weekly Report 2026-05-30

## Overview

During the week of 23–30 May 2026, UNESCO published several important items on climate change, biodiversity, and sustainable development. Key activities include advancing practical climate change education in Cambodian classrooms, highlighting indigenous knowledge guiding nature conservation in the Virunga region, and convening a global student event on innovation for sustainable futures. These initiatives reflect UNESCO's consistent approach of combining education, local knowledge, and community action to address environmental challenges and advance the Sustainable Development Goals.

## Teachers in Cambodia Prepare to Bring Climate Change Education into Classrooms

**Date:** 28 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO – Education for a Resilient and Sustainable Future: Strengthening Climate Change Education in Cambodia](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/education-resilient-and-sustainable-future-strengthening-climate-change-education-cambodia)

UNESCO reported on Cambodia's efforts to strengthen climate change education through the Climate Smart Education Systems Initiative (CSESI), funded by the Global Partnership for Education (GPE) and implemented jointly with Save the Children. While climate change topics such as weather patterns, deforestation, and renewable energy are already present in science, geography, and social studies curricula, the initiative is helping teachers move beyond theoretical lessons toward practical, hands-on learning and community-based climate action. UNESCO is also supporting evidence-based policy development, climate data integration, and teacher training to build a more resilient and sustainable education system in the country.

## Indigenous Knowledge and Traditional Practices Guiding Nature and Climate Conservation in Virunga

**Date:** 26 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO – Indigenous knowledge and traditional practices guiding nature and climate conservation Virunga](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/indigenous-knowledge-and-traditional-practices-guiding-nature-and-climate-conservation-virunga)

UNESCO highlighted how indigenous knowledge and traditional practices are guiding nature and climate conservation in the Virunga region of the Congo Basin. Local communities, scientists, Indigenous Peoples, site managers, and governments are working together within UNESCO-designated sites to protect ecosystems, conserve biodiversity, and promote sustainable livelihoods. The article demonstrates that local and traditional knowledge systems play an indispensable role alongside scientific approaches in maintaining ecosystem health and achieving the Kunming-Montreal Global Biodiversity Framework targets.

## Learning, Innovating, Acting: Building a Sustainable Future

**Date:** 28 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO – Learning, innovating, acting: Building a sustainable future](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/learning-innovating-acting-building-sustainable-future)

UNESCO hosted a Campus event bringing together 76 students from Benin, India, Nigeria, and the United Kingdom, along with 13 connected classes from around the world, to exchange ideas on innovation and sustainable development. Participants were encouraged to reflect on "smart" innovation that serves the common good, with the message that the value of technology should be measured not just by its performance but by its impact on society. The event emphasized that innovation must be inclusive and ethical, and reinforced UNESCO's education for sustainable development goals of building critical thinking, cooperation, and the capacity to act for a sustainable future.
