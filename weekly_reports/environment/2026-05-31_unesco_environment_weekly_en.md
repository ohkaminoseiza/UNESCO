# UNESCO Environment Weekly Report 2026-05-31

## Overview

The week of May 24–31, 2026 saw UNESCO active on two key environmental fronts. On May 25, UNESCO co-organized a regional forum in Dushanbe, Tajikistan on glacier and cryosphere monitoring in Central Asia, addressing the urgent water security crisis posed by rapid glacier retreat. On May 28, UNESCO published a feature on strengthening climate change education in Cambodia through the Climate Smart Education Systems Initiative. These activities sit within UNESCO's broader environmental mission: its network of designated sites (biosphere reserves, World Heritage sites, geoparks) spans over 13 million square kilometres, and the organization continues to advance science-policy interfaces for biodiversity and climate action worldwide.

---

## UNESCO Supports Regional Dialogue on Glacier and Cryosphere Monitoring in Central Asia

**Date:** May 25, 2026

On May 25, 2026, UNESCO supported the Central Asian Coordination Forum on Glacier and Cryosphere Monitoring, Assessment and Forecasting, held in Dushanbe, Tajikistan, as part of the 4th High-Level International Conference on the International Decade for Action "Water for Sustainable Development" 2018–2028. The forum brought together government agencies, scientific institutions, hydrometeorological services, and international partners including GIZ, IOM, ADB, and the Aga Khan Foundation to strengthen regional cooperation in cryosphere science, glacier modelling, and early warning systems. With shrinking glaciers and depleting snowpacks already threatening the water security of two billion people, and with Central Asia facing rapid glacier retreat and rising risks of glacial lake outburst floods, the forum underscored the urgency of coordinated regional action.

## Education for a Resilient and Sustainable Future: Strengthening Climate Change Education in Cambodia

**Date:** May 28, 2026

UNESCO published a feature on May 28, 2026 describing how the Climate Smart Education Systems Initiative (CSESI) is supporting Cambodia's Ministry of Education, Youth and Sport in mainstreaming climate change into teacher education. A UNESCO-supported mapping exercise revealed a significant gap between climate knowledge and actionable teaching skills, highlighting the need to empower educators to deliver real-world climate solutions. Since the project's inception in 2024, UNESCO has been developing evidence-based policies, climate data tools for educational planning, and teacher training resource packages for pre-service primary educators, bridging the gap between curricula and practical pedagogy.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/education-resilient-and-sustainable-future-strengthening-climate-change-education-cambodia](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/education-resilient-and-sustainable-future-strengthening-climate-change-education-cambodia)

## Upcoming: 38th Session of the MAB International Coordinating Council

**Date:** June 3–5, 2026 (announced this week)

The 38th session of the International Coordinating Council of UNESCO's Man and the Biosphere (MAB) Programme is scheduled to be held June 3–5, 2026. The council oversees the World Network of Biosphere Reserves, which spans over 100 countries and serves as living laboratories for sustainable development and biodiversity conservation. This session will discuss the expansion of the biosphere reserve network and review the implementation of the Lima Action Plan for UNESCO MAB and its World Network of Biosphere Reserves.
