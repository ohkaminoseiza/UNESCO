# UNESCO Environment Weekly Report 2026-05-16

## Overview

During the week of May 9–16, 2026, UNESCO's environmental activities centered on final preparations for the 3rd Global MAB Youth Forum (May 18–22, Italy), which will bring together up to 200 young people from Biosphere Reserves worldwide to shape the next decade of biodiversity and climate action under the Man and the Biosphere Programme. The UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning also widely promoted its upcoming Learning Cities Network webinar on local climate action, scheduled for May 20. UNESCO's coverage of the landmark IPBES Business and Biodiversity Assessment (published February 2026) continues to draw international attention to the systemic economic risks posed by biodiversity loss. The Hangzhou Strategic Action Plan 2026–35 for Biosphere Reserves, and ongoing efforts to strengthen science-policy interfaces at UNESCO-designated sites, reflect UNESCO's comprehensive approach to environmental governance at both global and local scales.

## 3rd UNESCO Global MAB Youth Forum 2026: "Committed to the Future We Want"

**Date:** May 18–22, 2026 (preparations and outreach concentrated in the week of May 9–16)

Italy will host the 3rd UNESCO Global Man and the Biosphere (MAB) Youth Forum from May 18–22, 2026, in the Po Delta Biosphere Reserve and Colli Euganei areas. Up to 200 young people aged 18–35, actively connected to UNESCO Biosphere Reserves around the world, will convene for an in-person exchange focused on biodiversity conservation, sustainable development, and community well-being. The forum aims to empower participants to shape the next decade of action within the MAB Programme and develop innovative solutions to address biodiversity loss and climate change. Food and accommodation costs are covered by the organizers; limited travel support is available.

## UNESCO Learning Cities Network Webinar: "Greener Cities, Stronger Communities — Advancing Local Climate Action"

**Date:** May 20, 2026 (widely promoted this week; 13:00 UTC)

The UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning (UIL) will host a Global Network of Learning Cities webinar on May 20, 2026, titled "Greener cities, stronger communities – Advancing local climate action." Cities are central actors in climate change response, and many UNESCO Learning Cities are integrating nature-based solutions, environmental education, and participatory greening initiatives to transform urban spaces while strengthening social cohesion. The webinar is part of the ongoing UIL series "Unlocking the power of lifelong learning," held every first and third Wednesday of the month, and brings together stakeholders from across the globe.

## Biodiversity Loss Poses Critical Risk to Businesses and Human Wellbeing — IPBES Report

**Date:** February 2026 (published); UNESCO coverage ongoing this week

The Intergovernmental Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES) released its landmark Business and Biodiversity Assessment in February 2026, and UNESCO has amplified the report's findings given their direct relevance to UNESCO-designated sites. Key findings: global finance flows exceeding $7 trillion in 2023 had direct negative impacts on nature, while only $220 billion was directed toward conservation and restoration. Fewer than 1% of publicly reporting companies mention biodiversity impacts. The report identifies over 100 actions for businesses, governments, and financial actors to better measure, manage, and reduce their impacts on nature — framing biodiversity loss as a systemic risk on par with climate change for financial stability and supply chains.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/new-report-signals-biodiversity-loss-poses-critical-risk-businesses-and-human-wellbeing](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/new-report-signals-biodiversity-loss-poses-critical-risk-businesses-and-human-wellbeing)

## Strengthening Science-Policy Interfaces in Biosphere Reserves and Global Geoparks

**Date:** Ongoing in 2026 under the Hangzhou Strategic Action Plan 2026–35

UNESCO is advancing a capacity-building initiative to strengthen science-policy linkages across its global network of Biosphere Reserves and Global Geoparks, recognizing that the full potential of these sites remains underutilized due to weak integration of site-based scientific insights into national and regional policymaking. The Hangzhou Strategic Action Plan 2026–35, adopted at the 5th World Congress of Biosphere Reserves, commits governments to strengthen conservation, sustainable development, and research. The World Network of Biosphere Reserves now protects 5% of the planet's surface — 784 reserves covering over 8 million km² — benefiting approximately 300 million people. Seven of the plan's 34 targets directly address Indigenous peoples and local communities, calling for free, prior, and informed consent and integration of traditional knowledge into governance.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/strengthening-science-policy-interfaces-biosphere-reserves-and-global-geoparks](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/strengthening-science-policy-interfaces-biosphere-reserves-and-global-geoparks)
