# UNESCO Environment Weekly Report 2026-05-10

## Overview

During the week of May 3–10, 2026, UNESCO's environmental activity focused on two published items: a May 8 update on the DIKTAS II project advancing groundwater governance across four Southeast European nations, and a May 8 article spotlighting journalist awards in Skopje recognizing excellence in reporting on climate change and disinformation. The week also saw an announcement for the 3rd Global MAB Youth Forum (scheduled May 18–19), which will engage youth in tackling biodiversity loss and climate change. Applications for the 2026 UNESCO Sultan Qaboos Prize for Environmental Conservation remain open through June 15. These activities reflect UNESCO's sustained multi-front approach to environmental protection: transboundary resource governance, media literacy on climate issues, youth empowerment, and recognition of outstanding conservation work.

## Regional Cooperation Advances Groundwater Governance in the Dinaric Karst

**Date:** May 8, 2026

UNESCO published a progress update on the DIKTAS II project — the second phase of the Global Environment Facility (GEF)-funded effort to implement the Strategic Action Programme of the Dinaric Karst Aquifer System. The project, jointly managed by UNESCO and UNDP, involves four Southeast European countries: Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, and Croatia, which collectively host some of the world's largest and most productive karst aquifers. A review meeting held March 16–17, 2026, in Trebinje, Bosnia and Herzegovina confirmed a key shift "from plans to action," with experts reinforcing coordination on transboundary groundwater protection, monitoring of dependent ecosystems, and gender-responsive governance frameworks. The project's ultimate goal is to safeguard drinking water supplies and strengthen ecological resilience across the region.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/plans-action-regional-cooperation-advances-groundwater-governance-dinaric-karst

## Shedding Light on Climate Change and Disinformation: Journalists Awarded in Skopje

**Date:** Published May 8, 2026 (Ceremony held April 30, 2026)

UNESCO published coverage of the annual media awards ceremony hosted by the Council of Media Ethics of Macedonia (CMEM) at the EU House in Skopje, North Macedonia. The event was held within UNESCO's EU-funded project "Building Trust in Media in South-East Europe: Support to Journalism as a Public Good" and recognized outstanding investigative journalism submitted to the competition "Facts and Disinformation on Climate Change." First prize went to Mihail Miloshevski (360 Stepeni) for reporting on North Macedonia's path away from coal dependency; second prize to Aleksandar Metodijev and Suzana Miceva (Nota.mk / TV Telma) for investigating illegal waste trafficking; and third prize to Valentina Vurmo (Lice v lice) for examining foreign waste contributions to pollution. The competition attracted 29 entries published between May 2025 and April 2026.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/shedding-light-climate-change-and-disinformation-journalists-awarded-skopje

## 3rd Global MAB Youth Forum 2026: "Committed to the Future We Want"

**Date:** Announced this week; event scheduled May 18–19, 2026

UNESCO announced the upcoming 3rd Global MAB (Man and the Biosphere) Youth Forum 2026, to be held May 18–19, 2026. The forum adopts the theme "Committed to the Future We Want," aligned with the Hangzhou UNESCO-MAB Strategic Action Plan 2026–2035. The forum aims to strengthen youth engagement in addressing the triple planetary crisis: biodiversity loss, climate change, and pollution. Participants — young representatives from UNESCO Biosphere Reserves and partner institutions worldwide — will share experiences and reinforce commitments to science-based sustainable development.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/3rd-global-mab-youth-forum-2026-committed-future-we-want

## Applications Open: 2026 UNESCO Sultan Qaboos Prize for Environmental Conservation

**Date:** Deadline June 15, 2026

UNESCO is accepting nominations for the 2026 Sultan Qaboos Prize for Environmental Conservation, awarded biennially to recognize exceptional contributions to the conservation and sustainable management of the natural environment. The prize includes a financial award of USD $100,000 and a certificate of recognition, to be presented at the World Science Forum in November 2026. Eligible candidates include individuals, groups, or institutions that have applied innovative and replicable approaches to environmental conservation, with particular attention to projects that engage local communities and align with the UNESCO-MAB programme's principles.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/applications-open-2026-unesco-sultan-qaboos-prize-environmental-conservation

## [Recent Context] Biodiversity Loss Poses Critical Risk to Business and Human Wellbeing

UNESCO recently amplified findings from a major report indicating that biodiversity loss has escalated from an environmental concern to a systemic economic risk threatening financial stability, global supply chains, and human wellbeing. UNESCO's Local and Indigenous Knowledge Systems (LINKS) Programme continues to support the integration of indigenous and local knowledge into biodiversity assessments conducted by the Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES). The report underscores that most businesses do not yet account for the true costs of their negative impacts on nature, accelerating decline and exposing themselves to growing operational and financial risks.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/new-report-signals-biodiversity-loss-poses-critical-risk-businesses-and-human-wellbeing
