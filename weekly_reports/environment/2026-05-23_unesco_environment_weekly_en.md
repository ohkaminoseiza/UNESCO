# UNESCO Environment Weekly Report 2026-05-23

## Overview

The week of May 16–23, 2026 was exceptionally active for UNESCO's environment, biodiversity, and climate-related work. The 3rd UNESCO MAB Youth Global Forum took place May 18–22 at Italy's Po Delta Biosphere Reserve, convening 200 young people from biosphere reserves worldwide to drive implementation of the Hangzhou Strategic Action Plan (2026–2035). On May 20, the UNESCO Global Network of Learning Cities hosted a webinar on urban climate action, exploring how cities can lead on climate resilience through lifelong learning and nature-based solutions. On May 22, International Day for Biological Diversity, UNESCO highlighted the essential role of its designated sites in safeguarding global biodiversity under the 2026 theme "Acting locally for global impact." UNESCO also convened a key expert meeting on integrating Indigenous and local knowledge into global biodiversity governance.

---

## 3rd UNESCO Global MAB Youth Forum 2026: Committed to the Future We Want

**Date:** May 18–22, 2026 (Po Delta, Italy)

The 3rd UNESCO Global MAB Youth Forum was held at the Po Delta Biosphere Reserve in Rosolina Mare, Italy, bringing together 200 young people aged 18–35 from UNESCO Biosphere Reserves around the world. Under the theme "Committed to the Future We Want," participants spent five days exchanging youth-led practices and shaping the next decade of action for biodiversity, climate, and sustainable development. The forum is aligned with the Hangzhou Strategic Action Plan (2026–2035), endorsed at the 5th World Congress of Biosphere Reserves (September 2025, Hangzhou, China), and focused particularly on Action Target 20 to develop the MAB Youth Network. Food and accommodation were provided by Italian organizers, with limited travel support available.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/3rd-global-mab-youth-forum-2026-committed-future-we-want](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/3rd-global-mab-youth-forum-2026-committed-future-we-want)

## UNESCO Learning Cities Webinar: Greener Cities, Stronger Communities — Advancing Local Climate Action

**Date:** May 20, 2026

On May 20, 2026, the UNESCO Global Network of Learning Cities hosted the webinar "Greener cities, stronger communities – Advancing local climate action," part of the UNESCO Institute for Lifelong Learning's new series "Unlocking the power of lifelong learning." The session spotlighted how UNESCO learning cities are integrating nature-based solutions, environmental education, and participatory greening initiatives to transform urban spaces while strengthening social cohesion. Cities were identified as central actors in climate response, and the webinar emphasized that local-level action by learning cities generates measurable contributions to global climate goals and the sustainable development agenda.

**Source:** [https://www.uil.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-learning-cities-webinar-greener-cities-stronger-communities-advancing-local-climate-action](https://www.uil.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-learning-cities-webinar-greener-cities-stronger-communities-advancing-local-climate-action)

## International Day for Biological Diversity (May 22): Acting Locally for Global Impact

**Date:** May 22, 2026

On May 22, International Day for Biological Diversity 2026, UNESCO highlighted its network of biosphere reserves, World Heritage sites, and UNESCO Global Geoparks as frontline tools for halting biodiversity loss. The 2026 theme, "Acting locally for global impact," resonates deeply with UNESCO's site-based approach: UNESCO-designated sites encompass more than 60% of globally mapped species—approximately 40% of which are found nowhere else—and store an estimated 240 gigatons of carbon, equivalent to nearly two decades of current global emissions. With over one million species now threatened with extinction, UNESCO emphasized that scaled-up local action through its global network of 784 biosphere reserves in 142 countries is essential to meeting the Kunming-Montreal 30×30 target.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/days/biological-diversity](https://www.unesco.org/en/days/biological-diversity)

## UNESCO Hosts Key Meeting with Leading Biodiversity Experts

**Date:** May 2026 (this week)

UNESCO convened a key meeting with leading biodiversity experts this week, co-hosting an Intergovernmental Science-Policy Platform on Biodiversity and Ecosystem Services (IPBES) workshop in collaboration with the Center for Indigenous Peoples' Research and Development (CIPRED). Discussions focused on centering Indigenous and Local Knowledge (ILK) in global biodiversity governance, recognizing that traditional and local knowledge systems provide irreplaceable insight for conservation strategies and ecosystem management. UNESCO, as the sole UN organization with a science mandate spanning both natural sciences and cultural heritage, is uniquely positioned to bridge scientific and Indigenous knowledge frameworks in addressing the twin crises of biodiversity loss and climate change.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-hosts-key-meeting-leading-biodiversity-experts](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-hosts-key-meeting-leading-biodiversity-experts)
