# UNESCO AI Weekly Report 2026-05-23

## Overview

During the week of May 16–23, 2026, UNESCO's AI-related activities centered on three themes: designing human-centered AI for democratic societies, advancing rights-based data governance for inclusive AI futures, and upholding human agency in judicial systems. On May 18, UNESCO published a piece on building human-centered AI for democratic governance, while a joint UNESCO–UNDP data governance training initiative reported the outcomes of its first cohort of government officials from 23 countries. UNESCO also contributed to the G20's Justice20 (J20) forum as a knowledge partner, reinforcing the principle that humans—not machines—must remain the final decision-makers in courts of law. With the 4th Global Forum on the Ethics of AI set for September in Riyadh, UNESCO's global AI governance agenda continues to build momentum.

---

## Designing Human-Centered AI for Democratic Societies

**Date:** May 18, 2026

UNESCO published a discussion piece on May 18 on the critical importance of designing human-centered AI systems that support, rather than undermine, democratic societies. Analyzing the rapid proliferation of AI and its impact on democratic processes and civil rights, the article underscores three pillars as indispensable: transparency, accountability, and human oversight. Grounded in UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence—the only global normative instrument on AI applicable to all 194 Member States—the piece offers concrete methodologies for governments to embed AI ethics into policy design, and has attracted broad attention as a reference document for international AI policy debates.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence](https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence)

## Governments Advance Rights-Based Data Governance to Unlock Inclusive AI Futures

**Date:** May 2026 (this week)

The outcomes of the first cohort of a joint UNESCO–UNDP capacity-building program titled "Data governance for inclusive digital and AI futures" were reported this week. Hosted through the ITU Academy and supported by the EU Global Gateway initiative, the training brought together nearly fifty government officials and policymakers from twenty-three countries across Africa, Asia, and the Arab States. Participants gained practical tools to design rights-based data governance frameworks that ensure AI serves the public interest and promotes equitable development. A second cohort is scheduled to launch in June 2026, expanding access for policymakers worldwide.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/governments-advance-rights-based-data-governance-unlock-inclusive-ai-futures](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/governments-advance-rights-based-data-governance-unlock-inclusive-ai-futures)

## Judges, Not Machines: J20 Charts a Human-Centered Path for AI in Justice

**Date:** May 2026 (this week)

As a knowledge partner of the G20's Justice20 (J20) forum, UNESCO submitted an issue brief establishing that in matters of justice, humans—not machines—must remain the final decision-makers. Chief Justices and delegates from the world's twenty largest economies affirmed that innovation and human rights can advance together in the courtroom, but only if courts embed transparency, explainability, and human oversight from day one. While AI is increasingly used for administrative functions such as translation, transcription, and drafting of correspondence, extending it to higher-stakes tasks such as drafting judgments requires careful ethical frameworks and robust accountability mechanisms.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/judges-not-machines-j20-charts-human-centered-path-ai-justice](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/judges-not-machines-j20-charts-human-centered-path-ai-justice)

## 4th UNESCO Global Forum on the Ethics of AI (GFEAI 2026) — Riyadh, September

**Date:** September 14–17, 2026 (announced)

UNESCO has reiterated its announcement that the 4th Global Forum on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (GFEAI 2026) will take place September 14–17, 2026 in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, co-hosted with the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Building on the 2021 UNESCO Recommendation on the Ethics of AI—the only global normative instrument on AI applicable to all 194 Member States—the forum will bring together governments, civil society, academia, and the private sector to advance ethical AI governance frameworks. More than 75 countries across five continents have now initiated or completed UNESCO's AI Readiness Assessment Methodology (RAM), reflecting strong global uptake of UNESCO's governance approach in the lead-up to the forum.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai](https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai)
