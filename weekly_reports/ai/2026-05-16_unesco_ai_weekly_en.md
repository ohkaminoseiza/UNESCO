# UNESCO AI Weekly Report 2026-05-16

## Overview

During the week of May 9–16, 2026, the most significant UNESCO AI development was the official launch on May 15 of the second edition of its MOOC on digital citizenship and artificial intelligence, targeting educators in Latin America with practical curricula on ethical AI use, disinformation, and digital rights. The UNESCO–Oxford University free global course on "AI and the Rule of Law," which opened on April 27, continues to attract registrations and remains the week's other headline initiative. The joint UNESCO–Thomson Reuters Foundation report published in late March—documenting a stark governance gap across nearly 3,000 companies—continues to shape international policy debates. Preparations for the 4th Global Forum on the Ethics of AI (September 2026, Riyadh) are also advancing, underscoring UNESCO's ongoing role as the global standard-bearer for AI ethics governance.

---

## UNESCO Launches Second Edition of Digital Citizenship and AI Course

**Date:** May 15, 2026

UNESCO officially launched the second edition of its free online MOOC on digital citizenship and artificial intelligence on May 15, 2026, via the UNESCO Open Learning platform. Designed for educators, school teams, and social actors with an interdisciplinary, Latin American focus, the course covers ethical AI use, combating disinformation, digital coexistence, rights protection, democratic participation, and critical thinking. Classes begin June 14, 2026 and conclude August 6, 2026. The course follows the successful first edition and aims to equip education practitioners for the realities of the digital age.

**Source:** [https://openlearning.unesco.org/courses/course-v1:UNESCO+ED-004+2026_T1_ENG/about](https://openlearning.unesco.org/courses/course-v1:UNESCO+ED-004+2026_T1_ENG/about)

## UNESCO and Oxford University Launch Free Global Course on AI and the Rule of Law

**Date:** April 27, 2026 (opened; registrations ongoing this week)

UNESCO, in partnership with the University of Oxford (Saïd Business School, Blavatnik School of Government, and Faculty of Law) and supported by the European Union, launched a free global online course on "AI and the Rule of Law." The English edition opened April 27, 2026, with French and Spanish editions scheduled for June 2026. Designed for civil servants, policymakers, and legal professionals, the course provides tools to navigate AI's implications for governance, justice, and public administration. It complements the earlier UNESCO–Oxford civil servants AI course developed through the SPARK-AI Alliance.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-and-university-oxford-launch-free-global-course-ai-and-rule-law](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-and-university-oxford-launch-free-global-course-ai-and-rule-law)

## Responsible AI in Practice: 2025 Global Insights — Nearly 3,000 Companies Surveyed

**Date:** March 31, 2026 (ongoing international coverage this week)

UNESCO and the Thomson Reuters Foundation published "Responsible AI in Practice: 2025 Global Insights from the AI Company Data Initiative," analyzing 2,972 companies across 11 sectors and multiple regions. Key findings reveal a significant gap between AI adoption and governance readiness: only 44% of companies have an AI strategy; only 10% are publicly committed to an AI governance framework; just 11% evaluate the environmental impact of their AI use; only 7% evaluate human rights impacts; and only 12% have a policy ensuring human oversight of AI systems. The report calls for urgent alignment between corporate AI practices and UNESCO's global Recommendation on the Ethics of AI.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/responsible-ai-practice-2025-global-insights-ai-company-data-initiative](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/responsible-ai-practice-2025-global-insights-ai-company-data-initiative)

## 4th UNESCO Global Forum on the Ethics of AI (GFEAI 2026) — Save the Date

**Date:** September 14–17, 2026 (Riyadh, Saudi Arabia — announced)

UNESCO has announced that the 4th Global Forum on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (GFEAI 2026) will take place September 14–17, 2026 in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia. The forum will build on UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on the Ethics of AI—the only global normative instrument on AI applicable to all 194 Member States—and will convene governments, civil society, academia, and the private sector to advance ethical AI governance frameworks. As of early 2026, more than 75 countries across five continents have initiated or completed UNESCO's AI Readiness Assessment Methodology (RAM), underscoring broad global uptake of UNESCO's governance approach.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai](https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai)
