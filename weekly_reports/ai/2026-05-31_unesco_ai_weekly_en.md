# UNESCO AI Weekly Report 2026-05-31

## Overview

During the week of May 24–31, 2026, UNESCO's AI-related activities focused primarily on ethical integration of AI in education. The most significant announcement was a May 28 article on advancing ethical and innovative AI integration in higher education across South Asia. Additionally, UNESCO's free MOOC "Educating in the age of artificial intelligence: Digital citizenship at school" (second edition) continued to be actively offered, supporting educators and learners in building AI literacy. UNESCO's ongoing work is anchored in the 2021 Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence, which provides a global framework for member states to implement responsible AI governance across policy areas including education, data governance, and human rights.

---

## Advancing Ethical and Innovative AI Integration in Higher Education in South Asia

**Date:** May 28, 2026

UNESCO published a news article on May 28, 2026 announcing efforts to advance ethical and innovative AI integration in higher education across South Asia. The initiative supports universities and educational institutions in the region to incorporate AI into teaching and research in ways that align with ethical standards set out in UNESCO's Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (2021). The work aims to equip higher education institutions with both the normative framework and practical guidance needed to harness AI responsibly.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/advancing-ethical-and-innovative-ai-integration-higher-education-south-asia](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/advancing-ethical-and-innovative-ai-integration-higher-education-south-asia)

---

## Educating in the Age of Artificial Intelligence: Digital Citizenship at School (MOOC)

**Date:** May 2026 (second edition launched May 15, 2026)

UNESCO's free online course "Educating in the age of artificial intelligence: Digital citizenship at school" continued its second edition run through May 2026. The MOOC offers a comprehensive approach to digital citizenship for educators, covering topics such as ethical use of AI, countering disinformation, protecting digital rights, democratic participation, and developing critical thinking. Available in English and Spanish, the course equips teachers and learners with practical skills to navigate the digital age safely and responsibly.

**Source:** [https://openlearning.unesco.org/courses/course-v1:UNESCO+ED-004+2026_T1_ENG/about](https://openlearning.unesco.org/courses/course-v1:UNESCO+ED-004+2026_T1_ENG/about)

---

## Upcoming: 4th Global Forum on the Ethics of AI (GFEAI 2026)

**Date:** Announced; event scheduled September 14–17, 2026

The 4th UNESCO Global Forum on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence (GFEAI 2026) will be held in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, from September 14 to 17, 2026. The Forum will bring together global leaders, policymakers, experts, and stakeholders to advance dialogue and promote actionable, sustainable approaches to AI ethics and governance. This event builds on UNESCO's ongoing work to support implementation of its global AI ethics framework across 194 member states.

**Source:** [https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai](https://www.unesco.org/en/forum-ethics-ai)
