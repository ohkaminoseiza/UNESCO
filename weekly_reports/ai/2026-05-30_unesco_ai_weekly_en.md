# UNESCO AI Weekly Report 2026-05-30

## Overview

During the week of 23–30 May 2026, UNESCO advanced several key AI initiatives. The organization promoted ethical AI integration in South Asian higher education, closed nominations for its ICT in Education Prize focused on AI-powered creativity, and continued supporting ethical AI use in newsrooms across South-East Europe. These activities reflect the ongoing practical implementation of UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence across diverse regions and sectors.

## Advancing Ethical and Innovative AI Integration in Higher Education in South Asia

**Date:** 28 May 2026  
**Source:** [UNESCO – Advancing ethical and innovative AI integration in higher education in South Asia](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/advancing-ethical-and-innovative-ai-integration-higher-education-south-asia)

UNESCO published news on advancing ethical and innovative AI integration in higher education across South Asia, covering countries including India, Nepal, Bhutan, Pakistan, Maldives, and Sri Lanka. The announcement emphasized the importance of maintaining ethical, human-centered approaches when integrating AI into higher education systems. Drawing on findings from UNESCO's Report on Digital Transformation in Higher Education in South Asia, the initiative provides practical guidance tailored to the region's specific challenges and opportunities.

## UNESCO ICT in Education Prize: Nominations Closed for AI Creativity Theme

**Date:** 29 May 2026 (nomination deadline)  
**Source:** [UNESCO – ICT in Education Prize: Call for nominations](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/unesco-ict-education-prize-call-nominations-open-projects-supporting-learners-expand-creativity)

UNESCO's ICT in Education Prize closed its call for nominations on 29 May 2026 (23:59 CEST), under the theme "Reimagining creativity and critical thinking with artificial intelligence." The Prize aims to recognize projects that demonstrate how teachers and learners in diverse contexts engage with AI systems to encourage critical thinking, imagination, and creativity. Applications from innovative educational projects worldwide were submitted ahead of the deadline.

## AI in the Newsroom: UNESCO Supports Ethical Integration in South-East Europe

**Date:** May 2026 (recently published)  
**Source:** [UNESCO – AI in the Newsroom: Supporting Ethical Integration in South-East Europe](https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/ai-newsroom-unesco-supports-ethical-integration-south-east-europe)

As part of the EU-funded project "Building Trust in Media in South-East Europe: Support to Journalism as a Public Good," UNESCO is actively promoting the ethical and transparent use of generative AI in media and social media platforms across the Western Balkans. Press and media councils from South-East Europe and Türkiye adopted a landmark "Regional Declaration on the Ethical and Transparent Use of Artificial Intelligence in the Media," committing to update codes of ethics, enhance cross-border cooperation, and raise awareness of AI's growing role in news production and dissemination.
