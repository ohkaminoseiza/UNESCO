## World Press Freedom Day 2026: AI and the Future of Media

**Date:** May 4–5, 2026

The World Press Freedom Day 2026 Global Conference in Lusaka, Zambia brought together journalists, digital rights advocates, technologists, policymakers, and youth leaders to discuss how "digital platforms, algorithms, artificial intelligence, and new information actors reshape freedom of expression, media independence, and public trust." A dedicated panel analyzed the governance challenges posed by AI in news production and dissemination, exploring practical ways to strengthen information ecosystems. UNESCO's flagship report released at the conference pointed to a historic 10% decline in freedom of expression globally between 2012 and 2024, with AI-enabled disinformation identified as a key driver of this deterioration.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/world-press-freedom-day-2026-conference-shaping-future-peace

## [Recent Context] UNESCO Launches AI in Education Observatory for Latin America and the Caribbean

**Date:** April 14, 2026

UNESCO launched the Observatory on Artificial Intelligence in Education for Latin America and the Caribbean, a regional platform designed to support governments in integrating AI into their education systems with a focus on equity, quality, and sustainable development. Key lines of action include generating regional evidence and reports, supporting the development of ethical and regulatory frameworks, and training teachers and policymakers. This observatory represents the first coordinated regional mechanism for monitoring and guiding AI adoption in education across the continent. (Published prior to this reporting week but noted as a significant recent development.)

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/observatory-artificial-intelligence-education-latin-america-and-caribbean

## [Recent Context] "Responsible AI in Practice" Report — UNESCO & Thomson Reuters Foundation

**Date:** Launched March 31, 2026 / Published April 13, 2026

UNESCO and the Thomson Reuters Foundation released "Responsible AI in practice: 2025 global insights from the AI Company Data Initiative," a pioneering study drawing on data collected from over 3,000 companies between July and November 2025. The report reveals widespread governance gaps: while many companies have adopted AI tools, most lack comprehensive ethics frameworks aligned with UNESCO's 2021 Recommendation on the Ethics of Artificial Intelligence. The findings call for stronger corporate accountability, mandatory disclosure of AI use, and human rights impact assessments across the AI lifecycle. (Published prior to this reporting week but noted as a significant recent development.)

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/articles/pioneering-report-thomson-reuters-foundation-and-unesco-sheds-light-way-3000-companies-approach-ai

## [Upcoming] AI for the Public Sector Training — Nairobi, Kenya

**Date:** May 19–22, 2026 (upcoming)

UNESCO and the ITU Academy will co-host a training program on Artificial Intelligence for the Public Sector in Nairobi, Kenya. The program targets government officials and public sector leaders seeking to apply AI technologies ethically and effectively. This initiative builds on UNESCO's broader capacity-building mission, which has already supported more than 70 countries in developing national AI strategies using UNESCO's readiness assessment methodology.

**Source:** https://www.unesco.org/en/artificial-intelligence/ai-public-sector
